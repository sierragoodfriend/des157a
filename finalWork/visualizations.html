Usability Tests

I had 6 of my peers and/or housemates play my GameOn studio 3 game with one another (2 people at a time).

I only user tested on people who have never looked at or played my game before and I didn't tell them anything about the game itself. I observed their gameplay, and asked them these questions afterwards:

"What did you think of the game overall? Did you enjoy playing it?"

"Was the gameplay clear? Did you have any trouble understanding what was going on?"

"Was the website easy to navigate? Was there anything you noticed wasn't working correctly, or that was confusing in terms of navigation or instruction?"

"Do you have any ideas for how I can make user experiences in this game better?"

The feedback I recieved was incredibly helpful. As mentioned before in previous critiques, almost every user strongly advocated for a "current score display" of sorts, so that users can know what their score will be if they pass without having to do that math themselves.

My friend Eva suggested that I have the "passing score" in the same place as the player score, slightly expanding and contrasting, and perhaps in a bold color like red. I like that idea and will experiment with it.

Another helpful critque is that the rules are clear, but very lenghty for such a simple game. I mentioned at this point that I'm considering having a tutorial option. All of the users agreed that this would be helpful, and believe I should replace the rules button with a tutorial option.

Moving forward, I want gameplay to be as clear, simple, and easy as possible. The game itself is rather simple, but it's surprising how much work it takes to make a simple game effective and easy to understand. The tutorial idea is a little daunting, but I believe I can do it!
